Departments

Roll Cage

The roll cage of an ATV (All Terrain Vehicle) is one of the most important parts as its strength and structural stability determines the safety of everything inside it, including the driver and powertrain components like engine, transmission and gearbox. Along with safety criteria, the roll cage is designed in a way that it remains light and gives maximum visibility to the driver while still abiding by the rules and regulations set by the competition organisers.

Powertrain

The drivetrain is the group of components of a vehicle that delivers power to the driving wheels. The function of the drivetrain is to couple the engine that produces the power to the driving wheels that use this mechanical power to rotate the axle. This connection involves physically linking the two components, which may be at opposite ends of the vehicle and so requiring a long propeller shaft or drive shaft.

Suspension

The unpredictable nature of off-road racing creates the need for a reliable and efficient suspension system. BAJA suspension must be engineered which should satisfy the following criteria: A suspension which will influence significantly on comfort, safety and manoeuvrability, contributing to vehicles road holding/handling and braking for good active safety and driving pleasure while also protecting the vehicle from damage and wear from force of impact with obstacles (including landing after jumping).

Steering

The primary function of the steering is to provide turning capabilities and give proper directional stability to the ATV. While competing in the challenging courses of BAJA, the ATV needs to have high stability even over the trickiest terrains. This calls for the need of a less effort but high response steering mechanism that helps the driver keep maximum control of the vehicle and overcome all obstacles. The steering geometry is set in such a way that the ATV has a smaller turning radius and gives less tire wear.

Brakes

In any vehicle as important it is to be quick and go fast around corners, jumps or straights another vital part that has to be considered is the ability of the vehicle to come to a stop from those high speeds, therefore the brake department of a vehicle is one of the most important departments. In various rally or endurance races around the world, a car having better brakes can often beat an otherwise more powerful car which has worse brakes as it allows for better, sharper cornering. In the BAJA competition where the general speed of the vehicle isn’t what is heavily contested and more of the focus is upon the manoeuvrability of our vehicle on the rough offroad terrain the brakes are as important as always to maintain proper control of the vehicle and ensuring proper locking of the wheels in tight scenarios such as bumpy turns, jumps and more.
